cast and crew

Robert Baratheon

Played by Mark Addy

Robert Baratheon is the King of the Seven Kingdoms of Westeros at the beginning of the series and a longtime friend of Eddard "Ned" Stark. He ascended to the throne after leading a rebellion against the Targaryen dynasty, driven by the abduction of his betrothed, Lyanna Stark, by Prince Rhaegar Targaryen. This event, known as Robert's Rebellion, resulted in the overthrow of King Aerys II Targaryen, also known as the Mad King. After Lyanna's death during the war, Robert married Cersei Lannister to solidify his claim to the throne and unite powerful families. Despite his status as king, Robert's reign is marked by his disinterest in ruling and his indulgence in pleasures, which eventually lead to political instability and his untimely death.

Tyrion Lannister

Played by Peter Dinklage

Tyrion Lannister, the youngest son of Tywin Lannister, is known for his wit, intelligence, and sharp tongue. Despite being underestimated due to his dwarfism, Tyrion proves himself as a shrewd political player. He serves as Hand of the King to Joffrey Baratheon and later as Hand of the Queen to Daenerys Targaryen. Tyrion navigates the treacherous politics of Westeros, often using his cleverness to survive and influence events.

Read More

Cersei Lannister

Played by Lena Headey

Cersei Lannister, daughter of Tywin Lannister, is known for her ambition, cunning, and ruthlessness. As the wife of King Robert Baratheon, she becomes the Queen of the Seven Kingdoms. Cersei's primary concern is the power and security of her family, particularly her children. She engages in numerous plots to maintain and expand her influence, ultimately seizing the Iron Throne for herself. Her reign is marked by a fierce determination to eliminate all threats to her rule.

Read More

Catelyn Stark

Played by Michelle Fairley

Catelyn Stark, wife of Eddard "Ned" Stark, is the Lady of Winterfell and mother to the Stark children. She is fiercely protective of her family and deeply loyal to the Stark name. After her husband is called to serve as Hand of the King, Catelyn becomes embroiled in the political intrigues of Westeros. She plays a crucial role in the War of the Five Kings, seeking alliances and justice for her family. Her actions are driven by a strong sense of duty and love for her children.

Episode Guide

01

Game of Thrones: Season 1

The first season of Game of Thrones introduces viewers to the intricate and brutal world of Westeros, where noble families vie for control of the Iron Throne. It begins with Eddard "Ned" Stark being summoned to serve as the Hand of the King to his old friend, King Robert Baratheon. As political intrigue, betrayal, and dark secrets unravel, the Stark family finds itself at the center of a power struggle that will shape the fate of the Seven Kingdoms. Meanwhile, across the Narrow Sea, Daenerys Targaryen, the last surviving heir of the overthrown Targaryen dynasty, begins her own quest to reclaim the throne. The season is known for its complex characters, unexpected twists, and the introduction of the series' hallmark themes of power, loyalty, and betrayal.

Read More

02

Game of Thrones: Season 2

The second season escalates the conflict known as the War of the Five Kings. Robb Stark continues his campaign against the Lannisters to avenge his father's death and secure the North's independence. In King's Landing, Tyrion Lannister takes on the role of Hand of the King, attempting to manage the political chaos and protect his family’s interests. Stannis Baratheon, King Robert's brother, claims the Iron Throne and gathers an army with the help of the mysterious priestess Melisandre. Across the Narrow Sea, Daenerys Targaryen faces the harsh realities of leadership as she navigates through the Red Waste and builds her strength in the city of Qarth. Her journey highlights her growth from a young, exiled princess to a formidable leader. This season is notable for its intense political intrigue, the introduction of new characters, and significant developments in the major plotlines.

Read More

03

Game of Thrones: Season 3

Season 3 is marked by significant shifts in power and alliances. The season opens with the aftermath of the Red Wedding, a shocking and brutal event that results in major losses for the Stark family. Robb Stark’s death marks a pivotal moment in the War of the Five Kings. Meanwhile, Daenerys Targaryen's influence grows as she conquers the city of Yunkai and continues her campaign to liberate the oppressed in Essos. Jon Snow, now infiltrated among the Wildlings beyond the Wall, faces challenges as he struggles with his dual loyalties. The season also explores the rising threat of the White Walkers, setting up a larger conflict that will have far-reaching implications. The intricate storytelling, dramatic twists, and character arcs make this season a standout in the series.

04

Game of Thrones: Season 4

In Season 4, the power dynamics in Westeros continue to shift dramatically. The season begins with the aftermath of King Joffrey’s death, which triggers a series of political maneuvers and conflicts. Tyrion Lannister, accused of Joffrey’s murder, faces a high-stakes trial that tests his resolve and survival. Daenerys Targaryen’s rule in Meereen is challenged by insurgents and the complexities of governance. Jon Snow’s leadership is put to the test as he defends the Wall against the advancing Wildlings. The season is marked by intense battles, character developments, and the ongoing struggle for power, culminating in significant changes in the political landscape of Westeros.

05

Game of Thrones: Season 5

The fifth season continues the series’ exploration of political and personal conflicts. In the North, Jon Snow rises as a leader in the Night's Watch, facing the challenges of maintaining order and dealing with the Wildlings. In King's Landing, the power struggle intensifies with the rise of the High Sparrow and his influence over Queen Margaery Tyrell and King Tommen Baratheon. Daenerys Targaryen faces mounting challenges in Meereen, including a rebellion and the complexities of ruling a city. Arya Stark’s journey takes her to Braavos, where she begins her training with the Faceless Men. The season emphasizes the theme of power and its consequences, with major developments that set the stage for future conflicts.

06

Game of Thrones: Season 6

Season 6 is a turning point in the series, marked by the resurrection of Jon Snow and his pivotal role in reclaiming the North. Daenerys Targaryen prepares for her return to Westeros, amassing allies and a fleet to support her cause. Cersei Lannister seizes power in King’s Landing through drastic measures, culminating in a dramatic and destructive series of events. The season also explores the complexities of identity and destiny as various characters confront their pasts and futures. The dramatic battles, key character arcs, and shifting power dynamics highlight the season’s importance in advancing the overarching narrative.

07

Game of Thrones: Season 7

In the penultimate season, the convergence of key characters and plotlines intensifies. Daenerys Targaryen arrives in Westeros and begins forging alliances while confronting the growing threat of the White Walkers. The season features high-stakes battles, including the epic clash at the Wall, and significant revelations about the nature of the threat facing Westeros. The struggle for the Iron Throne continues as alliances shift and characters face personal and political challenges. The season’s focus on the impending war between the living and the dead sets up the dramatic final conflicts of the series.

08

Game of Thrones: Season 8

The final season delivers the climactic conclusion to the series. The battle against the Night King and the White Walkers culminates in a dramatic showdown. As the final conflict for the Iron Throne unfolds, major characters face their destinies, and the series reaches its resolution with a controversial and impactful ending. The season wraps up the long-running storylines, providing closure to the intricate plots and character arcs developed throughout the series. The final episodes are marked by epic battles, significant character decisions, and a resolution that reflects the complex and often harsh world of Westeros.
